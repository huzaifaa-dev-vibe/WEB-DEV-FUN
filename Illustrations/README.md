# Illustrations

> Use illustrations to add personality and explain concepts. Don't use them as decoration.

---

## Purpose

Use illustrations intentionally: to explain, to brand, to delight.

## Prerequisites

- [UI/](../UI/), [UX/](../UX/).

## Learning Outcome

You can pick or commission illustrations that match your brand and serve a purpose.

## Dependencies

- None.

## AI Instructions

When adding illustrations:
1. **Have a reason.** Don't add for decoration.
2. **Match the brand.** Same palette, style, line weight.
3. **Don't use obvious stock illustrations.** They scream "template".
4. **Prefer custom** even if simple.
5. **Use SVG** for crispness at any size.

## Human Notes

### When to use
- Empty states (no data yet).
- Onboarding (explaining concepts).
- Error pages (404, 500).
- Hero sections (sometimes).
- Pricing pages (sometimes).

### When NOT to use
- Just to fill space.
- Because everyone else has them.
- When a photo would do better.

### Styles
- **Flat** — minimal, geometric. (undraw, Storyset.)
- **Line** — outline style.
- **Isometric** — 3D-ish.
- **Hand-drawn** — organic, personal.
- **3D render** — modern, premium.
- **Abstract** — patterns, shapes.

### Recoloring
Most open-source illustrations let you recolor to match your brand. Do it.

### Don't
- Use the same unDraw illustration as 1000 other sites.
- Mix styles.
- Use illustrations that look AI-generated (see [ANTI-AI-DESIGN/](../ANTI-AI-DESIGN/)).
- Use photos of "diverse team laughing at laptop".

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Generic illustrations everyone has.
- ❌ Mismatched styles.
- ❌ Wrong palette.
- ❌ No purpose.
- ❌ Using illustrations where photos work better.
